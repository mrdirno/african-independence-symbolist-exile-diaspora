## Entry Points for Contributors

**Start anywhere. Fork freely. No permission needed.**

### Story Entry Points

- Write a 'lost chapter' from Kwame Mensah's youth in 1940s London, detailing the moment a key symbolic act of resistance was planned.
- Create a series of letters between Ama in Kumasi and a friend, describing the chilling atmosphere after her father's disappearance, using mundane details to convey fear.
- Develop the backstory of Ebenezer Okoh: a scene from his first day in British court, navigating the tension between his wig/gown and his inner convictions.
- Write a monologue from the perspective of the 'sankofa' symbol on the compound wall, observing the family across three generations.

### Technical Entry Points

- Build an interactive map of Kofi's London, layering historical landmarks of the British Empire with hidden sites of African diaspora intellectual and political activity (like the Pendragon Hotel).
- Create a 'Symbolist Decoder' tool that allows users to input text (like Kwame's poetry or radio broadcasts) and highlights potential double-meanings, cultural references, and hidden messages.
